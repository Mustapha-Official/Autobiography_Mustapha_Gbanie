MY Autobiography

From Tungie Kenema — To The Whole Digital World

Chapter 1 — The Beginning

My name is Mustapha Gbanie. I was born on 20th May 2004, in Tungie Gorama Mende Chiefdom, into a loving middle-class family. My mother is Jeneba Gbanie, and my father is Kekura M Gbanie. I grew up and went through all my schooling — from primary level up to WASSCE — here in Kenema District, Sierra Leone.

From early on, I had a clear goal: I wanted to change my life and move my family from middle class to a higher, better standard of living. That focus made me choose to stay away from dating or romantic relationships — I knew those things could distract me or bring early responsibilities I wasn’t ready for. My biggest fear was taking on duties I couldn’t handle yet, and adding stress to my life when my family was still supporting me. So I put all my attention into learning and building my future, trusting that hard work would lead me where I wanted to go.

Chapter 2 — Discovering Technology

As I watched the world grow and change with modern technology, I realized: technology is the path to big success. That was when I decided to dive deep into this field. I started attending computer classes, and when I sat my exams for Introduction to Windows and Microsoft Word, I got excellent grades — that result gave me so much confidence and made me want to learn even more.

I planned to go further into PowerPoint and other programs, but at that time, my father encouraged me to go to university. I began my first year at Eastern Technical University of Sierra Leone, studying BSc Social Work. After one year, my father asked me to change my course. I wanted to study ICT — because my heart was already fully in technology — but the fees were very high. I worried it would be too hard for my father to afford, but he told me: “Just go, Allah is the one who provides everything.”

Chapter 3 — Learning With Limited Resources

Even before university, I already knew exactly what I wanted to be: a Web Developer. I started learning HTML fundamentals, and soon moved to CSS — I understood it very well, and loved every part of it. But there was one big challenge: I code entirely on my phone (Itel A56) — I have never used a computer or laptop until now.

My phone is old, slow, and the internet connection is often weak, which even paused my learning of JavaScript and PHP for a while. But I never gave up I kept going, learning every chance I got. At university, I realized the school didn’t offer the courses I really wanted, and there were no proper tools or computers to use. I found myself learning far more from online tutorials and my own research at home than I did in class. Still, I respected my father’s wish and kept attending classes — but whenever I had free time on campus, or late at night when everyone else was sleeping, I was busy coding and studying. That is how I mastered HTML and CSS, even with all the difficulties.

Today, I am proud of how far I have come. I am already visible on Google Search — a big dream come true — and I am now starting my own small business: building websites for shop owners and businesses around me, helping them grow and be known online too.
My journey hasn’t been easy — no laptop, slow phone, limited resources — but my passion is stronger than any challenge. My goal is simple but big: to become a great developer, create things that will help people all over the world, build a name that is known everywhere, and leave a legacy that will never fade.

I am Mustapha Gbanie — always learning, always building, always moving forward.
